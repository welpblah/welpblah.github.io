two cool programmers

2024-11-15
 •  cli, bash, javascript, astronomy

less than a minute reading time

A couple of interesting programmers I’ve come across this past year are Kevin Boone and Greg Miller.

Boone particularly for his Bash script to display the moon phase on some date. His most popular project is a command-line utility for extracting text from EPUB documents.

Miller for his Celestial Programming: Greg Miller’s Astronomy Programming Page. Some of his programs reference a book titled Astronomical Algorithms (Willmann-Bell, 1991) by Jean Meeus–interesting.

As someone who likes looking at the moon, I like the idea of extending Boone’s simple script with one or more of Miller’s highly technical programs. For instance–you know, so as to know when I should go out and about–Miller’s program for determining the position of the moon using an algorithm from the aforementioned book.
